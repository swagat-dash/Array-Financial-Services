//FAQ

Frequently asked questions

  • What is an IPO?

    An unlisted company (A company which is not listed on the stock exchange) announces initial public offering (IPO) when it decides to raise funds through sale of securities or shares for the first time to the public. People like to invest in IPO's as they ususally give whopping returns in a short span.

  • How can I apply for an IPO?

    You can apply for any IPO using a DEMAT account. If you don't have a DEMAT account, you can Click here to create your account now absolutely free without any hidden charges.

  • Do I need a DEMAT account for applying for IPO?

    Yes, you can apply for an IPO through ASBA facility of your bank. You don't need a trading account to apply for an IPO. However, you need a demat account for credit of shares if shares are allotted to you. So in the end, you need to have a DEMAT account.

  • Can I open DEMAT account online?

    Yes, most of the brokers now have moved to a digitised platform that allows you to open DEMAT quickly and efficiently without the hassle of manual form fill-ups. In fact, SEBI from March 2019 has barred the delivery of physical shares of listed companies. All scrips will now get transferred to your Demat in digitised format. Click here to create your account now absolutely free without any hidden charges.

  • What is the eligiblity criteria and what documents are required for DEMAT account?
    • Proof of Identity (POI) (Eg.: Driving license)
    • Proof of Address (POA) (Eg.: Passport)
    • PAN Card
    • 1 to 3 passport size photographs
    • Proof of Bank Account (Eg.: Cancelled cheque)
    • Proof of Income (For trading in derivatives such as F&O) (Eg.: Copy of ITR Acknowledgement)
  • Can a student open a DEMAT account?

    It is a common notion that you need to be at least 18 to open a Demat account, but in reality, anyone can open a Demat account and at any age. If you are a student, you can do it by submitting your PAN, ID proof, and by completing the KYC formality.
